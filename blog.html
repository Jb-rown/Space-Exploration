NASA's Artemis III Mission Targets 2027 Lunar Landing

Posted on May 1, 2025

NASA announced that Artemis III, the first crewed lunar landing since Apollo, is now scheduled for 2027. The mission will land astronauts near the lunar south pole, targeting water ice deposits for future exploration. Challenges with SpaceX's Starship HLS and Axiom's spacesuits delayed the timeline, but progress is steady.

James Webb Telescope Discovers Oldest Galaxy Yet

Posted on April 25, 2025

The James Webb Space Telescope identified a galaxy, JADES-GS-z14-0, formed just 290 million years after the Big Bang. This discovery, announced in April 2025, pushes the boundaries of our understanding of early universe formation, revealing unexpectedly massive and bright galaxies.

Mars Sample Return Mission Gains International Support

Posted on April 15, 2025

NASA and ESA have secured additional funding and international partners for the Mars Sample Return mission. The Perseverance rover's samples, collected from Jezero Crater, could reveal signs of ancient microbial life. The mission aims to return samples to Earth by 2033.
